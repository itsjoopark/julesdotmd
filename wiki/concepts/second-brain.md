# Second Brain

## Definition

A personal, externalized knowledge management system where an individual captures, organizes, and connects information across all domains of their life. The idea is that a well-maintained external system acts as an extension of biological memory — a "second brain" that retains, cross-references, and surfaces knowledge that would otherwise be lost or scattered.

## Key Points

- The term was popularized by Tiago Forte's "Building a Second Brain" methodology, but the underlying idea predates it by decades (see [[Memex]])
- Traditional second brains (Zettelkasten, personal wikis, Notion databases) suffer from a maintenance problem: the effort of organizing, linking, and keeping things current grows faster than the value, leading most people to abandon them
- The [[LLM Wiki]] pattern reimagines the second brain with LLMs handling all maintenance — summarizing, cross-referencing, filing, and bookkeeping — so the human focuses on sourcing, exploration, and thinking
- Common tools: Obsidian, Notion, Roam Research, LogSeq, DEVONthink

## Examples

- A personal knowledge garden tracking goals, health, psychology, reading notes, and self-improvement across years
- A research wiki accumulating papers, articles, and reports on a deep-dive topic
- A reading companion that builds character/theme/plot pages as you work through a book
- An internal team wiki maintained by LLMs, fed by Slack threads, meeting transcripts, and project documents
